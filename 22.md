# Project 22: Open Pathway Maps: Scalable and FAIR Visualization of Metabolic Networks

## Abstract

Many high-quality metabolic pathway resources provide rich, curated biochemical knowledge, but manually creating biologically meaningful pathway maps remains cumbersome. At the same time, several widely used pathway resources have moved to restrictive licensing models (e.g. KEGG, MetaCyc), limiting open access and reuse. As AI-driven analyses increasingly depend on high-quality reference knowledge, FAIR and openly accessible pathway representations are becoming more important for expert validation, interpretation, and programmatic reuse.
 
 This project will advance the systematic evaluation, benchmarking, and future automation of metabolic pathway maps by combining data-driven approaches with expert curation. We will benchmark existing layout strategies, including graph layout methods, constraint-based approaches such as UNICON, and standards-aware tools based on SBML and SBGN, while defining biological and visual requirements for pathway maps.
 
 The project brings together contributors from multiple ELIXIR-related resources and provides diverse datasets as input and evaluation benchmarks. Representative pathway maps from BRENDA, Reactome, Metabolic Atlas, and other open resources will serve as reference cases to assess layout quality and identify shared visual principles. Resulting pathway representations will be made available in structured, machine-readable formats to support downstream analyses such as omics data integration.
 
 All outcomes are designed for integration into sustainable research infrastructures, including the DSMZ Digital Diversity Hub, Metabolic Atlas, and ELIXIR Node services such as systems biology knowledge graphs supporting SBML and SBGN exports (e.g. the Stress Knowledge Map). This project aims to establish a scalable and FAIR framework for metabolic pathways relevant to systems biology, metabolomics, and plant science.
