# Project 19: Towards a semantic artefact catalogue for exposome ontology cross-mapping

## Abstract

The exposome represents the totality of environmental and endogenous exposures across an individual’s lifetime, encompassing both external and internal components. It has additional spatial and temporal features related to the location, duration, and timing of exposure.
 
 This complexity presents significant challenges for integrating exposome data with multi-omics datasets, given the diversity of data types, measurement modalities. Although several initiatives proposed a unified metadata standard and data model for exposome research, interoperability remains limited due to fragmentation across ontologies, disciplines, and regional practices.
 
 To address this, we propose a cross-ontology semantic integration approach rather than enforcing a single unified standard. In this project, we develop a semantic artefact catalogue (SAC) that aggregates artefacts across relevant domains, including environmental exposures, toxicology, clinical phenotypes, and lifestyle factors. We then implement a cross-mapping framework to align semantically related concepts across ontologies e.g. ECTO, ChEBI, ENVO, ExO, FoodOn … etc, enabling researchers to use domain-appropriate vocabularies while maintaining interoperability across datasets.
 
 We will use OntoPortal as the infrastructure for SAC with artefacts modelled as standardised entities derived from ontologies accessed via OntoPortal APIs. Cross-mapping will be limited to 10-12 ontologies and a prototype use case will be developed. 
 
 The project is aimed at expanding the activities of Toxicology Community work by applying the FAIRification efforts of the toxicology community to exposome. This will also include building collaborations with different exposome initiatives where INTOXICOM outputs/implementation approaches can be used. SAC will also help intercommunity collaboration due to its various artefact alignments, e.g. metabolomics and pathogen FG
