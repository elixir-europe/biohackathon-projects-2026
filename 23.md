# Project 23: Single-cell dataset integration across repositories and species for cell type homology analyses

## Abstract

Single-cell data are rapidly increasing both in quantity and diversity; however, clear reporting standards have not yet been adopted across the field. This leads to multiple non-compatible practices across datasets and resources, limits reusability, and makes it difficult to retrieve, integrate, and interpret data in a robust way. In parallel, comparative single-cell atlases are creating unprecedented opportunities to study cell type evolution across species. Yet robust cell type homology inference remains challenging because results depend strongly on upstream choices, including preprocessing, annotation granularity, and the homology relationships used to connect genes across species. 
 
 Several groups are already collaborating on these issues under the umbrella of the scFAIR consortium, including CELLxGENE, EMBL-EBI Single Cell Expression Atlas, Bgee, ASAP, Alliance of Genome Resources. We propose to leverage their foundational work regarding common metadata schema and aggregation of datasets across resources, and to extend it for improving multi-species analyses, and cell population matching. Our overall aim is to improve dataset integration across resources and species, to shed light on cell population determination mechanisms.
 
 During this BioHackathon, we propose to develop 3 work packages, benefiting from the diversity of skillsets among participants. I) biodata resource component: to develop adaptors to query and integrate datasets annotated in multiple ELIXIR biodata resources and beyond. II) Tool benchmarking component: to benchmark how pre-processing choices impact analysis results and mappability, notably across species. III) Cell biology component: to investigate homologous and lineage-specific cell types to identify cell innovations or incomplete atlas coverage.
