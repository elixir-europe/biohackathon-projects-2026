# Project 30: BiCoReCa (Biodiversity Community Resource Catalogue): Towards Centralized Curation and Integration

## Abstract

The rapid growth of biodiversity research has led to the development of numerous bioinformatics tools and databases, information about which remains fragmented across disparate cataloging efforts, hindering resource discovery and utilization. To address this gap, we offer the development of BiCoReCa (Biodiversity Community Resource Catalogue), a comprehensive, dynamic, open-access catalogue of biodiversity-related bioinformatics resources (tools, workflows, training, standards, and databases). Our team leverages successful prior ELIXIR BioHackathon experience, building upon lessons from developing Galaxy CoDex and a related initiative MiCoReCa. This demonstrates our capability to execute the BiCoReCa initiative and deliver impactful outcomes. The BiCoReCa catalogue's framework will be designed for transferability, enabling integration into platforms like Galaxy and other open-source initiatives.
 
 This initiative will utilize EDAM and cross-reference established registries like bio.tools and WorkflowHub to create a centralized, FAIR inventory. A key feature is the community-driven process for identifying and curating missing ontological terms and metadata, ensuring BiCoReCa's accuracy and relevance in collaboration with partner efforts. 
 
 Furthermore, the catalogue will integrate links to training materials from TeSS to support appropriate tool usage, and connect with OpenEBench for benchmarking capabilities. This project will provide a vital resource for the biodiversity field, enhancing research efficiency and reproducibility. It will also establish a sustainable, adaptable infrastructure potentially applicable to other communities, such as Single-Cell Omics in ELIXIR or image data workflows in Euro-BioImaging, who also showed interest in this initiative. This effort represents a significant contribution by the ELIXIR Biodiversity Community and the Tools Platform to streamline findability within biodiversity informatics.
