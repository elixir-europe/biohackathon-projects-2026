# Project 29: A gender-informed bibliometric index to amplify underrepresented voices in ELIXIR publications

## Abstract

Gender representation in scholarly publications remains a critical challenge in Life Sciences, where women authors, particularly as leading contributors, are systematically underrepresented. Bibliographic information retrieval systems such as Google Scholar, despite their widespread use, operate through opaque ranking algorithms that may further amplify such biases, reducing the visibility of female-led research. Building on work initiated at BioHackathon Europe 2024 and refined over the past two years, this project leverages a gender-aware bibliographic retrieval workflow developed by the Bioinfo4Women initiative at the Barcelona Supercomputing Center (BSC). The system automatically retrieves publication metadata and infers author gender, enabling systematic analysis of gender bias in scholarly visibility. The current project extends this foundation by introducing a novel bibliographic ranking index that incorporates gender as an explicit parameter alongside conventional metrics such as citation counts, h-index, and impact factor. The goal is to surface the contributions of underrepresented female authors across Life Sciences domains, allowing their work to emerge beyond what traditional ranking systems would otherwise permit. The use case is the curated set of >3,000 ELIXIR-supported publications compiled by the ELIXIR Impact Group. Bibliographic parameters will be retrieved efficiently through the BIP! Services (https://bip.imis.athena-innovation.gr/), developed by the ELIXIR Greece node. This effort directly aligns with ongoing ELIXIR Hub initiatives to systematically identify ELIXIR contributions across publications, projects, and training activities, and connects to BSC's sustained co-leadership of the ELIXIR ELEAD initiative focused on women's leadership in Life Sciences.
