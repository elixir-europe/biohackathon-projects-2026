# Project 28: WeatherXBiodiversity: Exploring Harmonization of Cross-Domain Data into FAIR Digital Objects

## Abstract

Climate change is one of the major challenges of the coming years, with particularly serious impacts on biodiversity. Understanding and responding to these impacts increasingly depends on combining data from different scientific domains, especially biodiversity observations and climate data. Both kinds of data are abundant and openly available, but they are not readily aligned: they use different metadata models, conventions, and publication pathways, making them difficult to combine in a reproducible and reusable way.
 
 At the same time, technologies such as RO-Crate now exist to package such multimodal data, workflows, and provenance into FAIR digital objects (FDOs). However, when assembling cross-domain research objects from heterogeneous sources, researchers need additional support with structuring, annotating, and harmonizing the metadata.
 
 This project will address that need by developing a reusable approach for combining cross-domain data into a unified FDO. The Annotated Research Context (ARC) framework will provide the working environment for organising data, workflows, and workflow executions, which can be represented as an RO-Crate for exchange and publication. As a concrete use case, we will combine bumblebee occurrence records from GBIF with ERA5 gridded climate data to reproduce the European component of Soroye, Newbold and Kerr (2020) on climate-driven bumblebee decline.
 
 The main outcome is not a new ecological result, but a reusable FDO, including empiric data, structured experimental and administrative metadata and executable workflows described through CWL. With the knowledge gained, we aim to deliver reusable templates, and accompanying training materials that others can adapt to related biodiversity–climate studies.
