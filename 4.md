# Project 4: Establishing a Flexible Open-Source Modeling System for Population-Scale Biology

## Abstract

Large biobanks contain vast amounts of genomic and phenotypic data that could help reveal the etiologies of prevalent clinical phenotypes such as Alzheimer’s disease or cancer. Despite the availability of these resources, they cannot be easily shared and combined across institutions due to privacy restrictions. We propose to establish a flexible open-source modeling framework that enables the community to combine publicly available biobank data and other datasets while preserving individuals’ privacy. In this project, we will build a network-based pipeline for integrating biobank data within knowledge graphs and further subtyping of individuals to identify etiological factors of complex diseases. We will extend our project from the ELIXIR BioHackathon 2025, in which we introduced a methodology for the modeling of genotype-phenotype interactions based on genomic hashes. We implemented it as a ready-to-use bioinformatics pipeline and showed that it is capable of fast and scalable genome comparisons using public data. Extending this work at the ELIXIR BioHackathon 2026, in collaboration with several European and international biobanks, will pave the way for responsible data federation across biobanks and support the development and administration of maximally effective targeted therapeutic strategies. In addition, it will allow us to implement our approach within a standardized infrastructure, ensuring accessibility and visibility for a number of important stakeholders.
