# Project 6: Implementing Credit and Recognition for Research Outputs Beyond Publications in ELIXIR

## Abstract

Research in the life sciences depends on many outputs beyond traditional publications, including datasets, databases, software, and workflows. These outputs result from a diverse range of specialized activities - such as data curation, software engineering, training - that currently lack structured technical pathways for formal recognition. However, the technical infrastructure for representing and recognising contributions to these outputs remains fragmented, limiting visibility for contributors and reducing incentives for sustainable resource maintenance. This project will develop practical technical building blocks for a credit and recognition framework for ELIXIR resources.
 
 The project has three linked activities: 
 
 1. We will identify, reuse, and enhance existing standards to define an extended vocabulary of contributor roles. This effort focuses on capturing the nuanced activities and expertise required to produce non-traditional research outputs, ensuring that these contributions are accurately represented and interoperable across ELIXIR services.
 
 2. We will develop a technical recognition model useful to the ELIXIR Platforms and implementation guidelines for resource managers, covering contribution events, provenance, attribution, aggregation, and reuse. 
 
 3. We will expand the integration of APICURON and BIP! Scholar with GitHub to transform raw signals (pull requests, issues, releases) into structured recognition events. This provides a direct credit pathway for any ELIXIR resource reliant on git-based contributions, e.g. software tools and training materials.
 
 Where feasible, we will also identify candidate KPIs to assess coverage, uptake, interoperability, and usefulness of the framework. 
 
 Expected outputs: 
 
  * Include a draft controlled vocabulary 
 
  * Implementation recommendations
 
  * A prototype APICURON-GitHub workflow for the discussed activities 
 
  * Initial KPIs to assess framework uptake
