# Project 16: MiCoKnowGraph: Designing a Microbiome Collaborative Knowledge Graph linking complex, multi-domain data

## Abstract

Microbiome research generates complex, multi-domain data, spanning genomic, imaging, metabolic, clinical, and environmental contexts. While recent initiatives have demonstrated the potential of knowledge graphs (KGs) to map bacteria-host interactions and microbiome-disease associations, these efforts remain fragmented and non-interoperable across disparate resources. To address this bottleneck, we propose the conceptual and architectural design of a unified microbiome KG, led by the ELIXIR Microbiome Community. This BioHackathon project focuses on foundational data modelling to integrate heterogeneous ecosystems and foster connections with Biodiversity, Health, Food & Nutrition communities.
 
 Given the complexity of mapping multi-omic, imaging, and other data, our primary objective is to collaboratively design a scalable, interoperable KG schema. Participants will identify existing KGs and essential entities, such as taxonomic units, (functional) genes, metabolites, diseases, and habitats, and define their formal semantic relationships by evaluating and mapping established ontologies (e.g., Gene Ontology, ENVO, DO, SOSA, SIO, NCIT, UBERON, EFO, DCT). The designed KG will be partly implemented in collaboration with the VERDANT proposed project.
 
 The community will evaluate this consensus-driven architecture by designing query templates for four use cases: aeromicrobiome functional characterisation, antimicrobial resistance transmission, host-microbiome interactions in cancer, and the metabolic impact of nutrition. By delivering a formalised blueprint, this project will empower researchers to harmonise existing KGs and unlock actionable insights from complex ecosystems.
