# Project 21: Supporting ELIXIR Nodes in maturing RDM services through the Data Stewardship Handbook

## Abstract

ELIXIR Nodes need practical guidance to consistently organise RDM services in a harmonised way. The Data Stewardship Handbook & Maturity Model (DSH) (https://elixir-europe.github.io/ds-handbook), currently a beta version, provides the starting point. However, many pages do not yet show how the guidance is applied or how issues are solved in practice. For example, pages on RDM training, expert networks or metadata describe approaches without showing how these are organised at the Node level. This project improves the uptake of DSH within the ELIXIR RDM ecosystem by strengthening the links to existing RDM resources and Platform activities (e.g., RDMkit, FAIR Cookbook, DSW).
 
 Participants work on selected Handbook pages and apply them to real situations from their Nodes. They revise content, clarify guidance and integrate examples that reflect existing practices. The page on RDM training, for example, will be developed into a structured page that includes a Node case and links to e.g. TeSS for resources and RDMkit for roles. The metadata page will include links to FAIRsharing for standards, the FAIR Cookbook for implementation recipes and DSW for DM planning support. This collaborative and practice-based approach enables participants with different levels of experience to work together on Handbook pages.
 
 The work remains within a clearly defined scope (see below) and delivers publishable updates. It results in reusable Handbook pages that enable Nodes - alongside international communities from multiple disciplines beyond ELIXIR - to move from general guidance to implementation, and to improve their RDM services based on practices tested across ELIXIR.
