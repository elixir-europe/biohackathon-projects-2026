# Project 18: Condeessi a Conda-EESSI transpiler for increased software stack portability, performance and resilience

## Abstract

Scientific software in the life sciences has been distributed through two major and complementary ecosystems: Bioconda (and Biocontainers) and EESSI, the European Environment for Scientific Software Installations. EESSI provides a shared software stack optimised for high-performance computing, designed for performance and portability across a wide range of processor architectures, from x86 and ARM to RISC-V, built on EasyBuild, a framework for building and installing scientific software on HPC systems.
 
 The urgency of bridging these ecosystems has grown sharply following Anaconda's 2024 licensing changes, which introduced commercial licensing requirements for any organisation with 200 or more employees, effectively restricting the use of Anaconda's use at many HPC centres. Major research institutions in Europe have begun phasing out (Ana)conda, accelerating the need for open alternatives. EESSI, built entirely on open-source foundations, is well-positioned to fill this gap. Simultaneously, Bioconda risks being affected by disruptions triggered by these changes.
 
 This project aims to develop Condeessi (pronounced "Condessi"), a transpiler tool that facilitates conversion of packaging recipes between the Conda and EasyBuild formats. The primary direction is Conda-to-EESSI, with bidirectional conversion as a longer-term goal. A prototype already exists and validates the feasibility of the approach, though it also reveals its technical complexity.
 
 Bioconda has amassed enormous value through thousands of carefully curated, community-maintained recipes. Rather than duplicating this effort in EESSI, Condeessi aims to multiply that investment across ecosystems. Errors or inconsistencies discovered during transpilation will be fed back to Bioconda and Biocontainers as upstream improvements, directly benefiting all three communities.
