# Project 8: Advancing Research Software Quality across ELIXIR: Sustainable, Reproducible, and Interoperable Life-Science Software

## Abstract

This project aims to evaluate and enhance research software (RS) quality dimensions, indicators, and best practices to support sustainable, reliable, and reproducible research across ELIXIR Nodes. Assessing RS quality remains a major challenge in the Life Sciences, where software plays a central role in data analysis, integration, and interpretation. At the same time, the principles underpinning RS quality are broadly applicable across the wider research ecosystem, including the European Health Data Space (EHDS) and other EOSC science domains. European initiatives such as B1MGplus, EVERSE, and ELIXIR-STEERS highlight the growing consensus on the need for coordinated approaches and provide a strong foundation for this work.
 
 The project will review, map, and prioritise research software quality indicators most relevant to the ELIXIR infrastructure and related initiatives, including the One Million Genomes (1+MG). It will build on existing community-driven efforts, including the quality dimensions and indicators developed during BioHackathon 2024, their further development in EVERSE, and the good practices promoted by ELIXIR-STEERS.
 
 Particular attention will be given to well-described metadata, compliance, risk assessment, and alignment across different software tiers to reflect varying levels of maturity. The project will also consider the broader operational context, including infrastructures such as secure processing environments, especially relevant to 1+MG and EHDS.
 
 This work is directly relevant to the ELIXIR Tools Platform, where efforts are ongoing to define and implement approaches for assessing software quality. The workshop will contribute to these activities by aligning proposed indicators with Tools Platform needs and strengthening connections with EVERSE.
