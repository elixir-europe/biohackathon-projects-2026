# Project 9: From alignment to insight: synchronized MSA and 3D structure visualization

## Abstract

Every biologist has stared at a multiple sequence alignment (MSA) and wondered: what do these conserved columns actually mean in 3D? For decades the answer required structural expertise or a painful context-switch into specialist tools. AlphaFold, ESMFold, and Foldseek have made high-quality structure ensembles available for virtually any protein family — yet the visualization paradigm hasn't kept up. We still compare structures the way we did 40 years ago: backbone superpositions, ribbon diagrams, cluttered ensemble views that experts navigate by instinct and non-experts simply cannot use.
 
 This project closes that gap. In five days we will build an open-source, browser-based tool that takes a standard MSA (FASTA or CLUSTALW) alongside protein structures and produces a synchronized, clutter-free view: conservation and divergence mapped directly onto molecular surfaces, with the 1D alignment and 3D view locked in sync. Conserved drug-binding pockets light up. Divergent interaction surfaces stand out. No structural expertise required.
 
 Building on the Mol*/iCn3D MolViewSpec bridge from BioHackathon Europe 2025, we deliver: (1) a one-click ensemble comparison demo across GPCRs, Tyrosine Kinases, and Immunoglobulins, (2) a reusable surface-mapping API, (3) a web UI where you load your own MSA and structures and get a publication-ready visualization in seconds, and (4) LLM-assisted alignment, analysis, and reporting — turning static comparisons into living narratives.
 
 If you work with protein families, drug targets, or AlphaFold clusters and have ever wished your alignment could show you the biology — this project is for you. Sequence biologists, structural bioinformaticians, tool developers, and students all welcome.
